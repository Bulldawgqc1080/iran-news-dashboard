# ClearView — Iran Conflict News Dashboard

A free, open-source news dashboard designed to help Americans (and anyone) cut through media bias and understand what's actually happening with the Iran conflict.

## Why This Exists

Most Americans get their news from outlets that have political, corporate, or national interests baked into their coverage. This dashboard doesn't pretend to be without perspective — instead, it **makes perspective visible**.

Every piece of content is labeled as one of three things:

| Label | Meaning |
|---|---|
| 🔵 **Verified Fact** | Independently confirmed by multiple credible sources |
| 🟠 **Official Statement** | A government or military claim — not yet independently verified |
| 🟣 **Media Framing** | An editorial angle, narrative choice, or opinion |

This distinction is what most news apps hide. Here, it's front and center.

---

## What's Inside

- **Overview** — Key facts, official claims, and media framing — filterable by type
- **Perspectives** — Side-by-side coverage comparison: U.S. media, Iranian state media, European press, and Middle Eastern outlets — including what each *emphasizes* and what each *omits*
- **Timeline** — Chronological events from 1953 to present, with historical context that mainstream coverage often skips
- **Sources** — Bias transparency ratings for every major outlet: ownership, known lean, and what each source is best used for

## How to Contribute

This dashboard is only as good as the information it contains. We welcome contributions that add verified facts, correct errors, or add new perspectives.

**Please read [CONTRIBUTING.md](CONTRIBUTING.md) before submitting.**

The short version:
- Fact submissions must include a primary source link
- Label your contribution correctly (Fact / Official Statement / Framing)
- No propaganda from any side — this includes U.S. government framing presented as neutral fact
- Cite original documents (UN transcripts, IAEA reports, CENTCOM briefings) over media paraphrases when possible

---

## Guiding Principles

1. **Transparency over neutrality** — No source is truly neutral. We show you the bias rather than hiding it.
2. **Facts first** — Verified facts are the foundation. Everything else is labeled accordingly.
3. **Context matters** — History doesn't start today. Events have causes that stretch back decades.
4. **No single narrative** — The goal is to show you multiple perspectives so you can form your own view.
5. **Open source** — This tool belongs to everyone. Fork it, improve it, translate it.
